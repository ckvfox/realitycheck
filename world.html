🌍 RealityCheck – World Trends Dashboard

The world is changing faster than ever — and not always for the better. Rising temperatures, biodiversity loss, conflicts, and shifting economic power reveal a planet under increasing pressure. This dashboard highlights global trends that raise concern but also provide insight into where humanity is making progress.

While national statistics offer valuable detail, only a global perspective shows how all these forces connect — from climate to conflict, from energy to environment. Understanding these linkages is essential to see where the world is headed.

Each chart below visualizes a core global indicator using data from Our World in Data. They serve as reality checks on our collective trajectory — and reminders of what still can change.

🌡️ Global Temperature Anomaly

This metric shows anomalies in global surface temperature relative to the pre-industrial baseline (1861–1890). It’s one of the clearest measures of the planet’s long-term warming trend.

🌍 Annual CO₂ Emissions (Fossil & Industry)

Total global CO₂ emissions from fossil fuels and industrial processes per year. Rising emissions remain the dominant driver of climate change.

🌋 Natural Disaster Events

The number of recorded natural disasters worldwide, based on EM-DAT data. It reflects both increased reporting and growing human exposure to climate extremes.

🐾 Global Living Planet Index

The Living Planet Index tracks biodiversity trends across thousands of vertebrate populations since 1970. Declines indicate how ecosystems and species are under stress.

⚔️ Battle-related Deaths (All Armed Conflicts)

Total deaths from all armed conflicts worldwide. Fluctuations highlight changing geopolitical tensions and humanitarian crises.

🔥 Global Warming Contribution from Historical Emissions

The cumulative contribution of fossil and industrial emissions to global temperature rise. It shows which activities have most shaped today’s climate conditions.
